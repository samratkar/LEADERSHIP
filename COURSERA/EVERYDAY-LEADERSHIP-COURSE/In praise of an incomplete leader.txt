HBR - In praise of an incomplete leaders.
1. Leaders are expected to know all. But then, it is not humanely possible. Today, leaders are not expected to command and control. Instead they are expected to *cultivate and coordinate* the actions of others at all levels of the organization. Only when leaders come to see themselves as incomplete—as having both strengths and weaknesses—will they be able to make up for their missing skills by relying on others.
